Travel to Tokyo!

Overview

The Japanese capital of Tokyo is known for its large skyscrapers, modern technology, and culture. The city mixes ultramodern and traditional architecture, incorporating different modern elements with Japanese culture. Tokyo is recognized for being the largest city in the world, with a population of 9.273 million people in 2015. Tokyo is known as a city that has shaped the way humanity looks at the future. It has grown vertically to accomodate all of its residents, and due to the low amount of available space Tokyo's architects are known for making use out of every space that is available. In Tokyo, the typical size of a one bedroom apartment is not more than 15 square meters. Before 1868, Tokyo was known as Edo. The lack of war and conflict at the time allowed Edo to use the majority of its resources to build and develop into a city. Tokyo's total GDP was $869 billion in 2015, and it's GDP per capita was at $64,269 that same year. In the future, Tokyo will host the 2019 Rugby World Cup as well as the 2020 Summer Olympics and Paralympics. Tokyo was ranked as first in a list of the 60 safest cities in the world, and third in the list of best cities for students. Its growing economy and infrastructure show no signs of weakening in the future, and it continues to be one of the most famous cities in the world.

Culture

Culture and education grew and matured during the Edo period of Tokyo. As previously mentioned, this period of time took place before the year Edo became known as Tokyo, or before 1868. Due to the large amount of cultural growth attributed and gained from this time period, Tokyo has ended up as being one of the most rich cities in the cultural aspect. Today, Tokyo is known for its historic shrines and temples, which are visited by many tourists around the world. Among them are the Meiji-jingu shrine and the Senso-ji temple. These are some of the most famous cultural destinations in Tokyo. Traditional culture has also had a large role in the fashion, design, and architecture of Tokyo, fusing cultural and modern elements together. Due to these reasons, Tokyo is known internationally for fusing culture with modern elements and creating a society that is very cultural in many different ways.

Advancements

Over the past decades, Tokyo has been leading the world into the future with its innovation and technology, making it a city known for its technological wonders. In the 2020 Olympics which will be hosted in Tokyo, facial recognition will be deployed for the first time in any Olympic event and robo taxis will roam the streets as ferries for tourists and athletes. Such technological advancements are only bringing more investment into the city and leading the world in technological innovation. These advancements show no sign of stopping, due to the increasing number of companies and tech-related companies that are filling Tokyo's business market. We can clearly see the reasons why Tokyo is considered as a leading city in technology and innovation around the world,and that these technological advancements are changing the world and adapting to different societies.
